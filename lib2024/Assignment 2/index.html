Some fun facts about frogs that you may not know


Frogs can jump up to eighteen feet from a sitting position. Frogs often have six or more legs. A frog croak can cause death in some species of snails. Certain species of Rain Frog (Froggus precipitatus) have developed rudimentary forms of flight. The Devil Frog (Froggus satanicus) gained its name from its tendency to strike one-sided deals with the newts that share its habitats. Frogs often eat meat, but they always prefer beef over chicken. If you meet six frogs on the road at night it is traditional to give them an offering of whatever is currently in your right pocket.

Leopard Frogs (Froggus spoticus) have developed a rudimentary form of sign language consisting of eye movements. The Maw Frog (Froggus mouthicus) is known for often carrying around packs of False Rain Frogs (Froggus perspiratus) in its large mouth. Many consider frogs to be amphibians, but they are actually divine. Scientists that study frogs are called Frogeteers and study the field of Frogetology. Never confuse a frog with the very similar species known as frugs; a mistake could be deadly.

Frogs have six life stags: egg, tadpole, tadpolepole, poletad, cocoon, toad, and frog. Frogs have three sets of lungs and eighteen sets of gills. Frogs can breath in mud by using their large toes. Frogs can climb on completely vertical and even upside down surfaces. Frogs have three tongues but use only one. If you hear creaking in your home at night, it is likely a Night Frog (Froggus nocturnicus). Take heart if you hear a Night Frog for that is considered good luck but be mindful not to gaze upon its majestic neon-coloured body or you may subject your family to a multi-generational curse.

Scientists estimate that within the next two years frogs will supersede humans as the dominant species on Earth and several other planets. If you have a pet frog be sure to mist them daily but only if they ask politely. Frogs have wildly varied diets including: insects, human blood, fruits, non-human blood, and tree nuts. Many species of frog enjoy being petted however some, such as the Rat Frog (Froggus rattus), do not. Many frogs have gruff demeanors but we should love them anyways. Frogs have webbed feet.
